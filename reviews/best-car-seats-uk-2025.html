Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need a car seat from day one?

Yes, absolutely. Car seats are legally required in the UK and essential for your child's safety. A car seat becomes more useful around 4-6 weeks when babies start to be more alert and parents need to travel.

Save money by buying the car seat you'll actually use for 1-4 years rather than an expensive "complete system" that becomes redundant after a few months.

What's the difference between i-Size and older standards?

i-Size is the newer, more stringent safety standard that requires rear-facing until 15 months and provides better side-impact protection. Older standards (ECE R44) are still legal but less safe.

The crucial difference: i-Size provides better safety and is future-proof, while older standards are being phased out. Many parents find i-Size more effective for safety because of the better protection.

How important is 360-degree rotation really?

Extremely important for your actual use. 360-degree rotation (like i-Spin) makes it easier to get your child in and out, especially in tight spaces. Fixed car seats can be frustrating and difficult to use.

If you primarily use the car seat for short trips, basic installation works fine. If you plan to use it frequently for daily travel, 360-degree rotation prevents constant frustration and physical effort.

Can I use a car seat for sleep?

No, car seats are not safe for sleep outside the car. Always follow the manufacturer's guidelines and current safety standards. Car seats are for supervised travel only.

Look specifically for car seats that meet current safety standards and avoid any that claim to be suitable for sleep. This limitation affects enough parents that it deserves consideration before purchase.

Should I buy second-hand car seats?

Absolutely yes, with safety checks. Car seats typically see 1-4 years maximum use before children outgrow them, meaning "second-hand" often means "barely used."

Check the car seat for proper operation, ensure all parts are present, and verify the installation works perfectly. Avoid car seats with damaged parts or signs of heavy use, as repairs often cost more than buying another second-hand alternative. We exclusively bought second-hand after our first expensive car seat, saving hundreds of pounds across multiple car seats.
