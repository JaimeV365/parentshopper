Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need a travel cot from day one?

Not necessarily. Many babies are happy in their parents' arms for the first few weeks. A travel cot becomes more useful around 4-6 weeks when babies start to be more alert and parents need to travel.

Save money by buying the travel cot you'll actually use for 1-3 years rather than an expensive "complete system" that becomes redundant after a few months.

What's the difference between a travel cot and a regular cot?

Travel cots are designed for portability with lightweight frames and easy setup, while regular cots are designed for permanent use with heavier frames and more features. Travel cots are better for travel, while regular cots are better for home use.

The crucial difference: travel cots prioritize portability and convenience, while regular cots prioritize comfort and features. Many parents find travel cots more effective for travel because of the lightweight design.

How important is weight really?

Extremely important for your actual use. Lightweight travel cots (like BabyBj√∂rn) are easier to carry and transport. Heavy travel cots can be frustrating and difficult to use, especially when traveling.

If you primarily use the travel cot for short trips, basic weight works fine. If you plan to use it frequently for travel, lightweight design prevents constant frustration and physical effort.

Can I use a travel cot for sleep?

Yes, travel cots are designed for sleep. Always follow the manufacturer's guidelines and current safety standards. Travel cots are for supervised sleep during travel.

Look specifically for travel cots that meet current safety standards and avoid any that claim to be unsuitable for sleep. This safety consideration affects enough parents that it deserves consideration before purchase.

Should I buy second-hand travel cots?

Absolutely yes, with safety checks. Travel cots typically see 1-3 years maximum use before children outgrow them, meaning "second-hand" often means "barely used."

Check the travel cot for proper operation, ensure all parts are present, and verify the setup works perfectly. Avoid travel cots with damaged parts or signs of heavy use, as repairs often cost more than buying another second-hand alternative. We exclusively bought second-hand after our first expensive travel cot, saving hundreds of pounds across multiple travel cots.
