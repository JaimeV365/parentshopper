What We Learned: Why Reliable Travel Cots Beat Fancy Features

Setup Mechanism Reality

Stiff mechanisms become stiffer over time, making setup frustrating at 2am in unfamiliar places. One-step setup models like the BabyBjörn save sanity, while straightforward assembly beats complex features every time.

Weight Matters More Than You Think

Carrying 15kg through airports while holding a baby is genuinely difficult. Ultra-lightweight options (under 7kg) are worth the premium for frequent travellers, while 8-10kg works fine for car travel.

Mattress Quality Varies

Not all travel cots include adequate mattresses. Some come with thin pads that need upgrading, while others include comfortable mattresses. Check mattress quality before buying, not just the cot features.

UK Space Reality

Small hotel rooms and car boots require compact folding designs. Briefcase-style cases are easier to store than bulky folded cots. One-step setup becomes crucial when space is limited.

What to Avoid: Lessons from Our Mistakes

❌ Complex Setup Mechanisms

Unless you enjoy wrestling with stiff mechanisms at 2am in unfamiliar places, avoid travel cots with complicated assembly. We learned this the frustrating way during our holiday disaster.

❌ Heavy Cots for Frequent Travel

If you travel frequently by plane or need to carry the cot long distances, avoid anything over 10kg. We hear from parents that 15kg+ cots become genuinely difficult to manage through airports while holding a baby.

❌ Cots with Poor Mattresses

Some budget travel cots include thin pads that need upgrading. For comfortable sleep, check mattress quality before buying—a good cot with a poor mattress defeats the purpose of travel convenience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need a travel cot, or can I use a regular cot?

We thought we could manage without until we actually travelled with a baby. Travel cots provide irreplaceable convenience: you can set up a familiar sleep environment anywhere, ensure consistent sleep routines, and avoid the stress of unfamiliar sleeping arrangements that can disrupt your baby's sleep.

Regular cots work for some families, particularly for longer stays, but travel cots pay for themselves in reduced travel stress and better sleep for everyone.

How heavy should a travel cot be for easy transport?

Weight matters more than you think. Our BabyBjörn at 6kg is genuinely portable—we can carry it through airports while holding our baby. The Hauck at 8.5kg is manageable for car travel but becomes heavy for frequent plane trips.

Ultra-lightweight options (under 7kg) are worth the premium if you travel frequently. Expect to pay more for lighter weight, but the convenience is worth it for active families.

Will travel cots work in small hotel rooms or car boots?

Most modern travel cots are designed for compact spaces. The BabyBjörn folds into a briefcase-style case, while the Hauck collapses to a reasonable size. However, some models like the Graco Pack 'n Play are bulkier when folded.

If you're staying in small spaces or have a small car, prioritize compact folding designs. The one-step setup models are particularly valuable when space is limited.

Are expensive travel cots worth the extra cost?

It depends on your travel frequency. We've found that premium features like one-step setup and ultra-lightweight design are worth the investment if you travel regularly. The BabyBjörn's premium price tag pays for itself in convenience.

For occasional use, the Hauck Sleep n Play at budget-friendly pricing delivers excellent value. The premium for luxury features comes with genuine convenience benefits that justify the cost for frequent travellers.

What about travel cot mattresses—do I need to buy separate ones?

Most travel cots include adequate mattresses. The Hauck comes with a decent included mattress, while the BabyBjörn includes a comfortable one. However, some budget options include thin pads that may need upgrading.

If you want extra comfort, you can buy separate travel mattresses, but most included ones are perfectly adequate for travel use. Focus on the cot's setup mechanism and weight rather than mattress upgrades.
