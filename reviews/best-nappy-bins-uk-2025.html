Frequently Asked Questions

Are nappy bins actually necessary or just marketing?

For families living in flats several floors from outdoor bins, or with bathrooms far from exits, nappy bins genuinely save time and reduce smell accumulation in regular bins. If you have easy outdoor bin access (ground floor home, attached garage), you can manage perfectly well taking nappies straight outside.

However, most parents making 6-8 nappy changes daily find dedicated bins worthwhile for convenience alone. At ¬£13-80 upfront, we'd say yes for most families.

Can I really use compatible refills in Tommee Tippee bins?

Absolutely yes. We've used Sangenic-compatible refills and generic "twist & click compatible" cassettes for 2+ years without issues. They fit identically, work identically, and contain smells identically. The only difference is packaging and price.

Search "Sangenic compatible refills" or "twist and click compatible cassettes" on Amazon ‚Äì you'll find multiple options at 50-60% less than official Tommee Tippee cassettes.

How often do nappy bins need emptying?

Depends on bin capacity and baby age:

  • Newborns (8-12 nappies daily): Every 2-3 days for Tommee Tippee (28-33 capacity), every 4-6 days for Ubbi/Korbell (45-50+ capacity)
  • 6+ months (4-6 nappies daily): Every 5-7 days for Tommee Tippee, weekly for Ubbi/Korbell
  • Twins: Double all numbers ‚Äì daily for small bins, every 2-3 days for large capacity

Do steel nappy bins really prevent smell absorption better than plastic?

Yes, objectively. Steel is non-porous while plastic is slightly porous at molecular level. Over months of use, plastic bins can absorb smell molecules into the material itself. This is why some plastic bins start to smell even when empty and freshly cleaned. Steel prevents this absorption entirely.

That said, well-maintained plastic bins (regular cleaning, not overfilling) perform adequately for most families. The steel advantage becomes more apparent over very long-term use (2+ years) or for subsequent children.

What about cloth nappy bins?

Cloth nappy families typically use different solutions than disposable users. Popular options include:

  • Bambino Mio Stay at Home Wet Bag (¬£15-20): Waterproof bag that holds 12-15 cloth nappies between washes
  • Basic plastic bins (like Rotho) with washable liners
  • Standard laundry baskets with washable nappy pail liners

Cloth nappies don't need the same odour-sealing technology as disposables because they're washed every 2-3 days maximum.
