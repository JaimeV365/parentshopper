Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need a bouncer from day one?

Not necessarily. Many babies are happy in their parents' arms for the first few weeks. A bouncer becomes more useful around 4-6 weeks when babies start to be more alert and parents need hands-free time.

Save money by buying the bouncer you'll actually use for 1-2 years rather than an expensive "complete system" that becomes redundant after a few months.

What's the difference between a bouncer and a swing?

Bouncers are powered by your baby's movement or gentle manual bouncing, while swings are powered by batteries or electricity and move automatically. Bouncers are typically more portable and take up less space.

The crucial difference: bouncers require some interaction from you or your baby, while swings can run independently. Many parents find bouncers more effective for soothing because of the human interaction.

How important is bounce quality really?

Extremely important for your actual use. Smooth bounce (like BabyBj√∂rn) soothes babies effectively and feels natural. Jerky or inconsistent bounce can actually make babies more fussy and frustrate parents.

If you primarily use the bouncer for short periods, basic bounce works fine. If you plan to use it frequently for soothing, smooth bounce prevents constant frustration and physical effort.

Can I use a bouncer for sleep?

No, bouncers are not safe for sleep. Always follow the manufacturer's guidelines and current safety standards. Bouncers are for supervised awake time only.

Look specifically for bouncers that meet current safety standards and avoid any that claim to be suitable for sleep. This limitation affects enough parents that it deserves consideration before purchase.

Should I buy second-hand bouncers?

Absolutely yes, with safety checks. Bouncers typically see 1-2 years maximum use before children outgrow them, meaning "second-hand" often means "barely used."

Check the frame for cracks, test the bounce mechanism thoroughly, ensure the harness works perfectly, and verify all recline mechanisms function smoothly. Avoid bouncers with worn-out covers or broken parts, as replacements often cost more than buying another second-hand alternative. We exclusively bought second-hand after our first expensive bouncer, saving hundreds of pounds across multiple bouncers.
