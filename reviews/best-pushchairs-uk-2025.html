Our Story: Four Pushchairs, Two Children, One Airport Disaster

We started like many first-time parents: convinced we needed the "perfect" travel system. We spent £400+ on our first pushchair, complete with car seat adapters, rain covers, and all the accessories the marketing promised we'd need.

Then reality hit. The pushchair barely fit in our car boot, required accessories that cost another £200, and was so bulky we could barely navigate through doorways. The "perfect" system became a daily frustration.

But we persisted, thinking we just needed to get used to it. This made everything worse. The weight, the bulk, the constant struggle to fold it properly—it was like trying to fit a wardrobe into a shoebox every time we went anywhere. We learned that pushchairs, like baby monitors, need to work for your actual lifestyle, not your aspirational one.

The final straw: We lost our beloved Chicco at an airport. Someone took it from the designated "pushchair collection area" while we were disembarking. The crew had directed all pushchairs to one belt, and by the time we arrived, ours had vanished. Someone clearly took it deliberately.

That's when we discovered the value of second-hand pushchairs—and everything changed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need a travel system from day one?

Not necessarily. Car seat adapters work brilliantly for the first 3-6 months, letting you click the infant car seat directly onto most pushchair frames. This keeps baby in the car seat from car to pushchair without waking them. After 6 months, you'll need the proper pushchair seat anyway.

Save money by buying the pushchair you'll actually use for 3+ years rather than an expensive "complete travel system" that becomes redundant after a few months.

What's the difference between cabin-approved and gate-check pushchairs?

Cabin-approved pushchairs (like BABYZEN YOYO2) meet size restrictions to store in overhead lockers, staying with you throughout the flight. Gate-check pushchairs get tagged at the gate and collected planeside when you disembark.

The crucial difference: cabin-approved pushchairs avoid the theft risk we experienced when someone took our Chicco from the designated collection area. Airlines can still gate-check cabin-approved pushchairs if overhead space fills up, but you have better odds of keeping it with you.

How important is wheel size really?

Extremely important for your actual walking environment. Big wheels (30cm+) handle countryside paths, parks, gravel, and bumpy terrain smoothly. Small wheels (12-15cm) work perfectly on city pavements and shopping centers but get stuck on every kerb and struggle on grass.

If you primarily walk in cities, small wheels save weight and bulk without sacrificing functionality. If you regularly walk on rough terrain, large wheels prevent constant frustration and physical effort.

Can tall parents use these pushchairs comfortably?

Most pushchairs reviewed here have fixed handles at approximately 105cm height. If you're over 6'2" (188cm), expect back discomfort during long walks with fixed-height handles.

Look specifically for pushchairs with adjustable handles (like some Bugaboo and iCandy models, though pricier) or test the height in-store before buying. This limitation affects enough parents that it deserves consideration before purchase.

Should I buy second-hand pushchairs?

Absolutely yes, with safety checks. Pushchairs typically see 2-3 years maximum use before children outgrow them, meaning "second-hand" often means "barely used."

Check the frame for cracks, test the brakes thoroughly, ensure the harness buckles work perfectly, and verify all recline mechanisms function smoothly. Avoid pushchairs with worn-out wheels or torn fabric, as replacements often cost more than buying another second-hand alternative. We exclusively bought second-hand after losing our Chicco, saving hundreds of pounds across multiple pushchairs.
